1. Histatin-3 (HTN3)

Length: 51 amino acids

Function:

Found in saliva

Kills germs like bacteria and fungi

Helps heal wounds in the mouth

Helps form a protective layer on teeth (called the enamel pellicle)



2. Where is Histatin-3 found?

It is secreted (released) by cells in the salivary glands into your saliva.



3. How many phosphorylation sites does human UTF1 have?

Human UTF1 has at least 4 known phosphorylation sites:

S18, S21, T35, and S42

These sites were found in lab experiments



4. Are these sites found in mouse UTF1 too?

Yes, these phosphorylation sites are conserved in mice.

The mouse version of UTF1 is very similar, with over 85% matching sequence, and it includes the same key sites.
